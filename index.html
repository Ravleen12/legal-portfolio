Contract Law

Lucy v. Zehmer

Excerpt of case material: “ … the manifestation of mutual assent requisite to contract formation is measured by an objective standard; the secret, unexpressed intent of a party is immaterial. A meeting of the minds is determined by the outward expression of the parties … ”

Tap to flip

Translation

Translation: If you act like you are serious, the law says you are. Contracts are about outward actions, not secret thoughts. (Two men signed a contract to sell a farm on a restaurant receipt; one later claimed he was “just joking.”)

Corporate Law

Dodge v. Ford

Excerpt of case material: “ … a business corporation is organized and carried on primarily for the profit of the stockholders. The powers of the directors are to be employed for that end. The discretion of directors is to be exercised in the choice of means to attain that end … ”

Tap to flip

Translation

Translation: Corporations exist primarily to make money for their owners (shareholders), not just for general social good. (Henry Ford wanted to use profits to help employees rather than pay dividends to shareholders.)

Litigation

Liebeck v. McDonald's: Hot Coffee

Excerpt of case material: “ … liability for defective products may attach under theories of negligence, strict liability, or breach of warranty. A defendant may be liable where it knew or should have known that its product posed an unreasonable risk of harm … ”

Tap to flip

Translation

Translation: This was not a frivolous case; it was about a company serving coffee at 190°F (which causes third degree burns) and refusing to fix the safety hazard.

Sarbanes Oxley Act

Hmong Veterans’ Service Act

The Status: Hmong soldiers who fought for the U.S. in the “Secret War” were denied benefits for decades.

The Clarity: This law simplified the path to citizenship, finally recognizing that “irregular service” in a hidden war counts as military service.

Intellectual Property

Happy Birthday Song Copyright

Excerpt of case material: “ … the licensor grants to the licensee a nonexclusive right to exploit the copyrighted work subject to proprietary royalty provisions, fee schedules, and accounting and audit rights … ”

Tap to flip

Translation

Translation: The court ruled the song belongs to the public. You cannot charge people a "royalty" for a song that has been around so long it belongs to everyone. (A company had claimed they owned the copyright to the song "Happy Birthday.")

Milestones in Clarity

Key moments in legal history that made the law more accessible.

1963

Gideon v. Wainwright

The Supreme Court ruled that states must provide lawyers to criminal defendants who cannot afford one, making legal representation a right, not a privilege.

1970s

The Plain English Movement

Lawyers and consumer advocates began pushing for clearer legal language in contracts, government forms, and consumer documents.

1966

Miranda v. Arizona

Police must inform people of their rights in plain language: the origin of “You have the right to remain silent”, so people can actually understand and use them.

2010

Plain Writing Act

U.S. federal law requiring agencies to use clear, straightforward language the public can understand. Legalese is no longer acceptable by default.
